Frequently asked questions
about pet insurance

What isn't covered by my pet insurance policy?

Any illness or disease that happens during the first 14 days of the policy or any condition that is present before the start date of the policy would not be covered. This includes recurrence of any illness, disease or injury that was present before cover started or that occurred during the first 14 days of your pet being covered.

It doesn’t cover any preventative, non-essential or routine treatments or tests, such as flea treatment, neutering and vaccination, behavioural problems, pregnancy, food and dental treatment (unless the dental treatment is due to accidental external injury) and any costs that, as a pet owner, you would normally expect to pay.

There is also no cover for any claim that results from an incident covered by other insurance, for example Third Party Liability.

Please refer to the policy wording for full details.

How do I make a claim on my pet policy?

Please call our Claims Hotline on 0845 246 8496 between 9am and 5pm Monday to Friday.

You will need to have the following information when you call:

  1. Policy number
  2. Pet's name
  3. Nature of claim

Please keep all receipts and invoices you want to claim for, and send them to us with your completed claim form and any other documents that support your claim.

If you have taken cover with us for trips abroad under the government’s Pet Travel Scheme, veterinary fee claims form must be completed at the time of treatment.

If my dog suffered from a condition that required long-term medication for more than 12 months, would I be able to continue the insurance with you?

We offer two levels of cover. Our Essential policy covers each separate condition for 12 months from the first date of treatment, up to a maximum of £4,000 per condition. This policy is suitable for unexpected accidental injuries or for acute conditions that are unlikely to require long-term treatment. Our Advanced policy covers each separate condition up to a maximum of £8,000, with no time limit on reaching this amount. The Advanced policy is suitable for on-going conditions such as arthritis or heart conditions. Treatment for the condition will be covered until the maximum benefit of £8,000 has been reached.

Once a pet is insured with us we will continue to insure it. We do not restrict cover or increase the excess due to a pet’s age. Please note that all cover for ongoing claims is subject to the policy remaining in force.

What is the excess on my pet insurance policy?

The excess is the part of the claim you must pay. For veterinary fees an excess applies once per condition and is currently £70 for cats and £80 for dogs. There is also an optional voluntary excess – currently £140 for cats and £160 for dogs. If you choose this optional excess, your premium will be lower.

There is an excess for material damage to property under section 7 - Third Party Liability. This is £250.

If you have any other excess applied to your policy this will be detailed in a separate endorsement letter or appear on your schedule.

At present we don't increase the excess as your pet ages. The excess is taken once per condition and is deducted from the claims settlement.

Do I pay the vet and you reimburse me, or can payment be made to the vet directly?

We are happy to send claim payments directly to your vet, as long as they are happy for us to do so. You can tell us who should receive the claim payment on the claim form. If you would like us to pay your veterinary practice directly, you should pay the excess to the practice first.

How do I make a claim on my pet policy online?

At the moment you can't make a pet insurance claim online but you can download our claims form. We're sorry if this causes any inconvenience. Alternatively, you can call us on 0845 246 8496.

If I don't make any claims on my pet insurance policy will I get a discount?

We don't offer a no claims discount on the premium for pet insurance. This is because we don’t want to discourage owners from taking their pet to see a vet and claiming for necessary treatment just to save money on their premium.

Do you change my excess as my pet gets older?

The excess is taken only once per condition and will be deducted from the claim settlement.

Are there any age limits or restrictions for pet insurance?

We can insure your pet as long as it is at least 8 weeks old and no more than 11 years old, (6 years for some breeds of dog), at the start date of the policy. If you start cover between these ages, we will not stop cover just because your pet has reached a certain age.

Why do your pet insurance premiums go up each year?

We take several factors into account when we calculate our pet insurance premiums. Each customer is rated according to his or her individual circumstances and risk.

These factors include:

  • The breed of your pet
    Some breeds are more susceptible to certain medical conditions. For example, some large breeds are more prone to bone and joint problems.
  • Your pet's age
    Just like people, older animals are more likely to suffer from certain medical conditions, such as heart disease, kidney problems and arthritis.
  • Where you live
    This can influence the level of vets' fees. These are the main reason people claim on their pet insurance and affect pet insurance premiums.

In order to help us keep our premiums down, certain things are not covered by the policy. One of these is the cost of having a pet put to sleep. However, we understand how distressing it is to lose a family pet and we offer a free pet bereavement service, staffed by professionals who can provide counselling and support.

Are hereditary and/or congenital conditions covered by the pet insurance policy?

Yes, as long as you were unaware of your pet's condition at the time you took out the policy. As with any illness or disease, your pet must not have shown any signs or symptoms of the condition beforehand or show any within the first 14 days of cover.

This excess will be deducted from the claim settlement. How much will it cost to insure my 7-year-old dog monthly?

Our pet insurance premiums take into account a number of different factors, such as the age and breed of your pet and where you live.

Currently we insure pets up to 11 years old (up to 6 for certain breeds of dog). Please go to our Pet homepage. From there you can find policy information and get a quote based on your individual details.

What can I do if I'm not happy with my pet insurance policy?

Before you take out a policy, read the policy wording and make sure you are happy with the cover it offers. Once you have taken out cover you have 14 days to decide whether it meets your requirements. If you decide to cancel in this period, we will give you a full refund, provided no claims have been made. After 14 days, you can cancel the policy at any time, although we won’t refund any premiums you have already paid. This is because, unlike other insurance policies, cover is paid for on a rolling monthly basis, so you will remain covered until the end of the last month you have already paid for.

How long does it take to settle a pet insurance policy claim?

Naturally, some claims are simpler than others. When you call us, one of our Claims Advisors will give you an idea of how long it should take. Provided the claim is within the terms of the policy and we have all the information we need, we will try to settle it promptly.

What is the difference between the 'Essential Cover' and the 'Advanced Cover'?

They give you a choice of cover for vets’ fees:

Our Essential policy covers each separate condition or associated condition up to a maximum benefit of £4,000, for a maximum period of 12 months from the first date of treatment, whichever limit is met first.

Our Advanced policy covers each separate condition or associated condition up to a maximum benefit of £8,000, with no time restriction on reaching this.

Within both the Essential and Advanced vet fee limit there are maximum fee levels for some treatments. For more information please see the policy wording.

Am I able to insure my pet if it suffers from a pre-existing condition?

The policy does not cover any conditions or symptoms that your pet suffered from before you took out the policy and before cover was in place. Or for any claim that is connected in any way with a pre-existing condition.

Who should I telephone in the event of a claim on my pet insurance policy?

Please call our Claims line, 0845 2468 496.

Can the premium be paid as an annual lump sum payment?

No. This is a policy that runs monthly and can only be paid by monthly Direct Debit. The monthly premium applies for 12 months.
