How will I know if my claim is covered by my policy?

Wherever possible, we’ll let you know during your first call to us whether your claim is covered by your policy. Before you call us, read through the policy booklet along with your schedule of cover– it might help you find the answer. If you’re still unsure, call our Claims Advisors. They have an excellent knowledge of the cover you’ve arranged with us.

Will my insurance premium increase at the next renewal date if I make a claim?

Claims under your policy could result in an increased premium at your next renewal date. This depends on the number, type and value of claims you make. Our premiums are calculated individually and you’ll be notified in advance of the renewal date.

Is my policy 'new-for-old'?

Yes. This means that all items will be replaced with the new equivalent. For example, if you’ve damaged a 5-year-old TV, we’ll replace it with the latest available equivalent model.

What questions will I be asked when I call to register my claim?

We’ll ask you what happened, when, and what action you've taken so far. We’ll ask you what you’re claiming for and the approximate cost(s) involved.

To report a claim, call 0845 246 8412. You’ll also need:

  • Your policy number.
  • The full address of the property covered under your policy.
  • A brief description of what happened and where the incident took place.
  • A list of the damaged items, including their original cost.
  • We’ll also ask you for a crime or loss reference number, if you’ve been a victim of theft, vandalism or lost items when outside of home.
  • Any relevant documents or photographs to support your claim.

Are the contents of my garage covered?

Yes. The items in your garage are covered under your Contents section of your policy as long as your sum insured is adequate. Theft of items from your garage will be limited to £2,500 under Home Insurance or £5,000 under Home Insurance Plus.

Does home insurance cover me for accidents?

Accidental damage cover isn’t included as standard. But you can choose to buy it and add it to your policy.

  • Our Home Insurance Buildings cover includes accidental damage to service pipes and cables as standard. Cover for wider accidental damage can be bought as an optional extra. This will cover accidents such as putting your foot through the ceiling.
  • Our Home Insurance Contents cover does not include accidental damage. But it can be bought as an optional extra. This will cover accidents such as spilling paint on the carpet.

Good to know: our Home Insurance Plus policy offers full accidental damage cover as standard.

Do I get a refund if I cancel my home policy?

When you buy your policy

If you cancel your policy before it’s due to start we’ll return any premium you’ve paid in full. If you cancel your policy within 14 days of the day it’s due to start, or on the day you receive your documents (whichever is later), we’ll return any premium you’ve paid minus an administration fee of £37.10 (inclusive of Insurance Premium Tax where applicable), providing you haven’t made any claims during that time.

If you cancel your policy after that 14-day period, we’ll return any unused premium minus an administration fee of £37.10 (inclusive of Insurance Premium Tax where applicable), providing you haven’t made a claim during the current period of insurance.

For more details on our charges, please check your policy schedule.

When you renew your policy

If you cancel your policy within 14 days of receiving your documents, or of the start date of the policy (whichever occurs later), we’ll refund the premium you’ve paid in full, providing that you haven’t made any claims during that time. If you cancel your policy after that time we’ll refund any premium paid for the remaining period of insurance minus an administration fee of £37.10 (including Insurance Premium Tax where applicable), providing that you haven’t made any claim during the current period of insurance.

Do I need to keep damaged goods for my claim?

Yes, we might need to inspect them. Also keep hold of any evidence of ownership such as receipts and photos or videos that will also help us deal with your claim.

What is policy excess?

This is the part of each claim that you pay. Our Claims Advisors will let you know how much excess you’ll need to pay. You can also find this information on your policy schedule.

We’ll take off the policy excess off the settlement of your claim, or alternatively our appointed specialist or supplier will collect this amount from you on our behalf.

Moving home soon - should I get a new policy or can you change on my current policy?

Give us a call us on 0845 246 3564 and we can change your policy. Remember if you’re selling your home, the buyer will be covered under the Buildings section of your policy up to the date the sale completes.

We have a national network of approved specialists and suppliers who we may appoint to inspect damage or replace goods on our behalf. Full details of the specialist or supplier and the next steps will be discussed with you by one of our Claims Advisors over the telephone.

We also have a team of Claims Advisors, who may visit you at home to discuss your claim in detail and will manage the settlement of your claim for you.

Who will deal with my claim once it has been reported?

You’ll be told which claims service unit will be dealing with your claim at the end of your first call to us. The details and contact numbers will appear on any written correspondence we might send to you.

What do I do if my pipes burst?

  • Turn off the water at the main stopcock or meter.
  • Switch off the central heating, immersion heater, etc.
  • Turn on all your taps to drain the system.
  • If water has flooded wiring or electrical systems - don’t touch them before arranging a professional inspection. Turn off the electricity at the mains.
  • Call professional tradesmen - plumbers, electricians, etc. - to make repairs.

And most important of all - contact us immediately to register your claim and get further guidance on what to do next!

Will my policy include any excesses?

You can select a policy excess for Buildings and/or Contents of between £50 - £500. This excess applies to all areas of cover, except subsidence and escape of water.

Subsidence claims are subject to an excess of £1000, unless a higher amount is stated in your policy schedule.

Escape of water claims are subject to an excess of £350, unless a higher amount is stated in your policy schedule.

Please check your quote for our current default excess.

Can I claim for storm or flood damage?

Our Buildings and Contents policies protect your home against the effects of storms and floods. Your buildings policy covers damage to buildings, garages and sheds and your contents policy will protect the items within your home and garden. Damage to hedges, gates and fences are not covered for damage caused by floods or storm.
