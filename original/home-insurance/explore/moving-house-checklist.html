Moving house
Checklist

We know moving home can be a stressful and difficult time for everyone. Our guide takes you through the process of moving - from the moment you make your mind up to move until the day you move to your new home.

Start checking

Start checking

2 months before
  • If you're renting, confirm your moving date with your current landlord.
  • If you're selling or buying, get all the legal bits and bobs in place early.
  • Time to check your home insurance - make sure it will cover your move.
  • Get all valuable possessions assessed for insurance.
  • If you're not covered, your removal company should be able to provide cover.
  • Transfer your youngsters' school records.

6 weeks before
  • Make a full inventory of your possessions.
  • Decide what to take to your new home and where you'll put them.
  • Start getting rid of things you no longer need. You can give them to charity, hold a car boot sale, or give them to your friends.
  • Get removal quotes and references from local companies - check their cover limits.
  • If you're not using professional removers, ask friends and family to help.
  • Book extra storage space if required.

1 month before
  • Organise to have your new house professionally cleaned.
  • Don't forget to clear out lofts, sheds, and storage rooms and outside spaces.
  • Notify utilities companies and switch home insurance to your new home address.
  • Change the relevant direct debits and standing orders.
  • Register to a new GP and dentist if you're moving out of the area.
  • Arrange for your post to be forwarded.
  • Send out change of address e-mails and messages to friends and family.
  • If you need furniture or carpets for your new home order them now and arrange delivery for when you move in.

2 weeks before
  • Finalise all the important details with your removals company and estate agent– confirm times, contact numbers, parking and directions.
  • Arrange for child and pet care on your moving day – ask trustworthy friends and family to help out.
  • Book additional services if your removals company is not providing them – such as disassembling/reassembling furniture and un-plumbing the dishwasher.
  • Cancel local services and memberships and settle outstanding bills.
  • Go through your cupboards, fridge and freezer and make sure there's no food left before you move out.

1 week before
  • Arrange a time to collect the keys for your new home from the estate agent or new landlord.
  • Gather all the keys from your old home for the new owners or tenants.
  • Make arrangements to pay for your move. Ask for receipts of any deposit paid.
  • Defrost the fridge and the freezer – they should be completely empty and dry before moving.

On the day you move out
  • Take notes or photos of all utility meter readings for water, electricity and gas.
  • Make sure the removal team knows which boxes and items are fragile – use labels.
  • When the movers have completed packing, check your inventory again – if everything on it is accounted for, just sign and you're ready to go.
  • Good idea: Walk around the house for one final check.
  • Hand over all the sets of keys to the new owners or the estate agent.
  • Make sure all windows and doors are locked and utilities are switched off.

On the day you move in
  • Get the kettle on, have some biscuits and enjoy a break with the removal team, your friends and family before you start unpacking.
  • When you're ready, give instructions to the removal team what needs to be moved and where.
  • Once again, account for each item on your inventory. Make sure you're happy with everything before the removal team leaves.
  • Did you read the utility meters? Write them down or take pictures.
  • Check you have all the keys you need.
  • Take a deep breath – you've made it.
