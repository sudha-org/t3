Our life insurance products

We offer two types of cover: Life Insurance and Over 50s Life Insurance. Both are designed to pay out a cash sum to help protect your family if you die.

Life Insurance

Cover that’s easy to set up from just £6 a month. With Life Insurance you choose how much cover you want and the length of your policy. This is a no-fuss life cover that will pay out a cash sum to the ones you care most about if you die during the policy term.

How it works


  • Level term assurance

    The benefit amount remains fixed throughout the length of the policy - unless you change your policy. Level term assurance is designed to help protect your family financially. It can pay out a cash sum if you die during the policy term – and your loved ones can use it however they wish. Level term assurance can also help cover an ‘interest only’ mortgage.


    Key features:

    • Premiums start at £6 per month, and stay fixed throughout your policy, unless you make changes to your policy.
    • Your cash sum payout stays the same, unless you make changes to your policy.
    • You choose how much you want to be covered for. If you don’t know, try our life insurance calculator.
    • Terminal illness cover could pay out your chosen amount of cover if you're diagnosed with a terminal illness and have a life expectancy of 12 months or less. This cover isn’t available during the last 12 months of your policy or for terms of less than two years.
    • You can add critical illness cover for an extra cost when you take out a life policy, giving you extra protection. This covers 41 conditions.
    • Our Guaranteed Insurability Option lets you increase your cover as your circumstances change - for example if you get married, enter into a registered civil partnership or move house.

  • Mortgage decreasing term assurance

    Designed to help cover a repayment mortgage - capital and interest. Your cover reduces roughly in line with your outstanding mortgage amount, but your premiums stay the same. This may be cheaper than a level term assurance policy.


    Key features:

    • Premiums start from £6 a month and stay fixed throughout your policy, unless you change your policy.
    • Terminal illness cover could pay out your chosen amount of cover if you're diagnosed with a terminal illness and have a life expectancy of 12 months or less. This cover isn’t available during the last 12 months of your policy or for terms of less than two years.
    • You can add critical illness cover for an extra cost when you take out a life policy, giving you extra protection. This covers 41 conditions.
    • Our Guaranteed Insurability Option lets you increase your cover as your circumstances change - for example if you get married, enter into a registered civil partnership, or move house.
    • If you’re buying a house or a flat, you can get free life cover between exchange of contracts and completion.

  • You choose who will benefit

    Make sure you know who will receive the benefit amount if you die. But if you make a terminal illness or critical illness claim we’ll pay the money to you.

    If you die, the payment will go to:

    • Your spouse/partner if you have a joint policy with them.
    • Your estate, unless you have let us know that you want the payment to go to a trust.
    • A trust, which is a legal arrangement that allows you to give away your assets immediately, without losing control. You must make sure the trust you choose meets your needs.

    If you want to learn more about trusts, please get professional advice. We’re not qualified to give advice. Keep in mind that you may be charged a fee for this. If you want to go ahead with a trust arrangement, please contact Legal & General.

Optional extra


Critical Illness
Cover

Include this in your policy at outset for an extra cost and you’re covered for a range of conditions that could disrupt your life and work. Being diagnosed with a critical illness is life-changing and this cover helps you manage the financial consequences.

While life cover can only pay out if you die during the policy term, critical illness cover could pay out your chosen amount of cover if you’re diagnosed with one of the conditions covered by the policy during the policy term.

Key features:

  • Critical illness cover can be added when you take out all our life insurance policies for an extra cost.
  • It covers 41 conditions in total.
  • Conditions covered include illnesses as stroke, heart attack and certain types of cancer. We only cover the critical illnesses we define in our Guide to Critical Illness Cover and no others.
  • If you’re eligible for a critical illness payment, the payment will be made directly to you.
  • Your children will also be covered for no extra charge. For more details on children's critical illness cover please read your policy’s terms & conditions.

Please bear in mind that Critical Illness Cover isn’t the same as terminal illness cover. Terminal Illness Cover could only pay out if you're diagnosed with a terminal illness and have a life expectancy of 12 months or less. Critical Illness Cover may pay out whatever your life expectancy during the policy term.

  • This isn’t a savings or investment product and has no cash value unless a valid claim is made. Inflation may reduce the value of your cover.

How it works


  • Guaranteed acceptance for 50 to 80 year olds living in the UK

    Our Over 50s Life Insurance has a guaranteed acceptance policy as long as you’re aged between 50 and 80, and you’re a UK resident.

  • You won’t need a medical or health assessment

    At Direct Line we understand that choosing over 50s life insurance can be challenging as well as confusing. That’s why we try to keep it simple. With our Over 50s Life Insurance you won’t need to attend any medical or health assessment.

  • Your loved ones could get a cash sum after just one year

    Taking care of your family after you’ve gone it’s easier than you think. A cash lump sum will be paid on your death after just one year so they can use this money to cover any outstanding bills or expenses – making their life simpler.

  • Fixed premiums starting from £8 a month

    Our monthly premiums are fixed, so you'll always know how much you're paying.

  • Putting your policy into a trust

    You can place the policy in a trust if you prefer. A trust is a way of giving away something of value for the benefits of others, but without giving them full access to and control over the policy. If you wish to proceed with a trust arrangement, please contact Legal & General.

Good to know

Make sure you think whether a trust is the right option for you. Remember it's your responsibility to make sure the policy meets your needs.

Optional extra


Funeral Benefit Option

We also offer an extra £300 contribution towards your funeral costs with the Funeral Benefit Option. With this option your cash sum will be paid directly to a Dignity funeral director, and they’ll help arrange the funeral with your family when the time comes. We know it’s not easy to think about your funeral now. But if you plan ahead, you can help your loved ones through difficult times.




What you need to know

  • Premiums stop after your 90th birthday but you still enjoy cover for the rest of your life.
  • A claim can be made after one year, regardless of the cause of death. If you die from natural causes within the first year we’ll refund any premiums paid, unless you die as the result of an accident, when we’ll pay your cash sum.
  • Depending on how long you live, the total sum you pay in premiums may be more than the cash sum payable on death.
  • If you stop paying premiums before the end of your policy, your cover will stop 30 days after your missed premium and you won’t get anything back.
  • This isn’t a savings or investment product and has no cash value until a valid claim is made.
  • Inflation will reduce the buying power of your cash sum in the future.
  • You can take out more than one policy as long as the total of all the initial premiums added together is not more than £50 a month. This includes any existing Legal & General Over 50s policies.
