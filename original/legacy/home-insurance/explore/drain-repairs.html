Who's responsible for drain repairs?

You know pretty quickly if your drain gets blocked. The toilet won't flush and grates overflow. Of course you'll want to get it fixed right away, but don't pay for expensive repairs if you don't have to. Changes to the law mean you're only responsible for your own drains.

What's changed?

For years, homeowners were legally required to maintain shared so-called 'private' sewers within their property - as well as some drains outside the property boundaries.


That's no longer the case. As of October 2011, water and sewerage companies are responsible for these repairs. Don't expect the water companies to shout about it though.


For more information see Department for Environment Food and Rural Affairs


What you're responsible for

Whatever anyone tells you, you're only responsible for your own drains - the ones used just by your home. Even if your drain meets a neighbour's, you're only responsible for your part of the drain.

Water companies are responsible for:

  • Repairs to all shared sewers - even if they're on private land
  • Repairs to all drains beyond private property boundaries

Report a blocked drain

If a blocked drain is causing problems you can contact your council's environmental health department. You can also contact the water company.
