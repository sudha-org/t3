Frequently Asked Questions
about our 7 day car repair service

What is the Direct Line Car Insurance 7 day repair service?

If we can't fix your damaged car in 7 days, we'll pay you £10 a day, until the job is done, up to £70.

What isn’t included in the 7 day repair service?

We won’t be able to offer you the 7 day repair service or £10 a day payment in these situations:

  • If your car is beyond repair and is classed as a 'write off'
  • If you decide not to use one of our approved repairers.
  • If your repair is only for windows or windscreen damage. These will be fixed by a specialist windscreen repairer who does not offer this service.
  • If you live in Northern Ireland, the Isle of Man, the Inverness area and some other remote areas in Scotland. If you’re in these areas and need to make a claim you'll be advised by our claims team if there's an approved repairer who can provide this service. If not we might be able to offer you an alternative repairer, but the repairs could take longer than 7 days and we won’t be able to offer you with the £10 a day payment if that happens.
  • If you request extra work to your car that isn't part of your claim, you might not be eligible for the 7 day service.

How does it work?

To use this service, you'll need to use one of our approved repairers. We have a nationwide network of approved repairers, all of which carry a British Standard Kitemark. All of our approved repairers work to the highest industry standards and all repairs are guaranteed for 5 years.

After your car's been picked up or dropped off at the repairer, they'll log it onto their system. When this is done, you'll receive a text (SMS). This is the start of your 7 days.

Your car will be inspected and any parts will be ordered. You might have already been asked to send in photos or videos of the damage, and if so, your parts might have been ordered already.

The repairer will let you know when they think your car should be ready.

You can keep up-to-date with the progress of your repair by logging into our repair tracker.

When the repair is complete you'll receive a text with the time and date. This is the end of your 7 days.

The repairer will then arrange an ideal time to return your car or for you to pick it up if that’s more convenient.

When does the 7 day repair time start and finish?

Our 7 day repairs are based on 7 full, 24 hour days in a row. This includes weekends, but doesn't include public holidays.

Once it's agreed that repairs can start on your car and it's arrived on-site, it will be logged on to the repairer's system. This will be confirmed to you by text and this is when the 7 day clock starts.

The repair time ends when your repairs have been completed. The repairer will send you a text with the date and time the repairs are completed.

If there's an issue with the authorisation of your repair after your car arrives at the repairer, this might delay your repair and might temporarily stop the 7 day repair clock.

Evening, weekend and public holiday car recovery

If your car isn't driveable and the incident happened overnight or when the approved repairer isn't open, we'll still collect your car. All you need to do is call us and we’ll sort this out for you.

 

It's likely that in these cases we'd store your car in a safe depot until the next working day. We'll then arrange for your car to be taken to one of our approved repairers. Of course, we'll call you to let you know exactly what's happening and give you all the details you need.

 

Once your car arrives at our approved repairer, we'll let you know. This will be the start of the 7 days, so you'll receive a text message that will include the date and time it arrived too.

What happens if the repair takes longer than 7 days?

We aim to repair all cars in 7 days, but we know that some repairs will take longer depending on the type of damage. We will never compromise on safety in order to achieve the 7 day repair time and all our repairs will meet manufacturers specifications and come with a 5 year guarantee. Sometimes there might be delays in getting particular parts which could also cause delays to the repair.

Whatever the reason, if we haven't managed to repair your car in 7 days, from day 8 we'll give you £10 for each day we're over, up to a maximum of £70. So even if your repair takes 1 minute longer than 7 days, you’ll be eligible for a payment.

If this happens the repairer will keep you informed. You won't have to do anything we'll handle everything. More often than not we'll be able to pay the money straight into the policyholder’s bank account and we'll aim to do that within a week of the repairs being complete. If for some reason we can't do this, don't worry we'll send a cheque instead – this could take a little longer to receive in the post. If you haven't received your payment within 4 weeks, please contact our claims team and we'll investigate for you.

How do I keep up to date with my repairs?

Your repairer will keep you informed and let you know if there are any changes to the date they think the repairs will be finished by. You can also use our online repair tracker to keep up to date and find the contact details for your repairer. You will be sent a text (SMS) with your log on details and your unique passcode. Your repairer can also provide you with your log on details.

All information about your repair shown on the Repair Tracker (including your personal information) is updated by the approved repairer who is responsible for making sure that the information about your repair is as accurate as possible.

All personal information shown on the Repair Tracker was given to the approved repairer by us so that they can arrange the repair of your car. The Direct Line data protection fair processing notice can be found in your policy booklet.

Your repair could be delayed if your contact details are wrong and the approved repairer can't contact you. You'll need to tell the approved repairer if any of your contact details change. You'll also need to tell us. Just contact our claims team if any contact details change as providing these details to the approved repairer will not update those held by us.

Your policy excess information has been provided to the approved repairer by us. If you think it's wrong then you'll need to contact us.

Do I need to send you photos to get the 7 day repair service?

No - if the repairer thinks it will help them assess the damage to your car and speed up the repair (for example, they might be able to order the relevant parts before your car comes in), they'll ask you if you're happy to send them photos or a video. Don't worry if you don't want to do this, that’s not a problem, you will still be able to use the 7 day repair.

If you're happy to send photos, the repairer will send you a link via text or email. All you need to do is click on this link and it'll take you through what you need to do – you can add a video as well as photos. Once you've sent these you'll see a note to say that the information has been submitted. Your repairer will then be able to look at the images and assess the damage to your car. Your repairer might have already arranged a suitable date with you for your car repairs to start. If not, they'll be in contact within 2 working days of receiving your photos to arrange a date with you. The repairer will always do a full inspection of the car when it arrives on site in case there’s any other damage that can't be seen from the photos.

What if my car is a write off?

If your car is beyond repair and is classed as a 'write off', we won’t be able to offer you the 7 day repair service or the £10 a day payment.

Instead, we'll make you an offer based on your car's market value or we'll help you find another car. There are some terms and conditions that relate to this, so you'll need to check with our claims team who will go through the options with you.
