Looking after your cat

How to look after your kitten - Hints and Tips

There’s more to owning a cat than having a fun, cute and fluffy companion. It is also a big responsibility. Take a look at our expert advice and tips on how you can keep your feline friend healthy and happy.

Diet

  • Food plays a vital role in your cat’s health. A cat needs a diet that contains essential nutrients such as proteins, fibre and fats to ensure they stay healthy.
  • A good quality, ready-made food will help to provide a well balanced diet.
  • Unlike dogs, cats are ‘nibblers’ and prefer to graze throughout the day.
  • Also, make sure that your cat always has access to a bowl of fresh water.

Well-being

  • Keeping your cat well fed, properly wormed, regularly treated against parasites and fully vaccinated is important.
  • Grooming, stroking and playing with your cat will help build a bond and strong relationship with them.

Playing and Hunting

  • Kittens and cats need to play, miming hunting and predation.
  • Playing with a paper or cloth toy is a positive way for your cat to be active and express her natural instincts.
  • It’s also important you join in these games so it can learn to control its biting and scratching.

Health

  • Cats are very clever at keeping an illness well hidden, until it gets serious.
  • If you notice a change in behaviour or habits, ask your Vet for advice.
  • Look out for signs such as loss of appetite, excessive drinking, vomiting, diarrhoea and scratching.

Cat-proof your home and garden

  • Before you get a cat or a kitten, scan your home for potential dangers to your new feline friend’s health and safety.
  • Always check ovens, washing machine drums or spin dryers. They are amusing hiding places – especially for small kittens.
  • Keep all heavy and hazardous items including sewing needles, books and vases out of reach.
  • Many plants are toxic to cats, so please be aware.
